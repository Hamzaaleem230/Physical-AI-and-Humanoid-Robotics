# References & Further Reading

This section provides a curated list of academic references, seminal research papers, and recommended books and online resources for deeper exploration of the topics covered in "Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics." Adhering to the book's principle of rigor and traceability, these resources form the intellectual backbone of our exploration.

## 1. Foundational Robotics & ROS 2

*   **Books:**
    *   Quigley, M., Conley, K., Gerkey, B., Faust, J., Foote, T., Saito, J., ... & Berger, E. (2009). *ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System*. ICRA workshop on open source robotics.
    *   Correll, N. (2022). *Programming Robots with ROS 2: A Practical Guide to the Robot Operating System*. O'Reilly Media. (For comprehensive ROS 2 practicals).
    *   Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016). *Springer handbook of robotics*. Springer. (A comprehensive theoretical foundation).
*   **Online Resources:**
    *   **ROS 2 Documentation:** [https://docs.ros.org/](https://docs.ros.org/) - The official and most up-to-date resource for ROS 2.
    *   **URDF Tutorials:** [http://wiki.ros.org/urdf/Tutorials](http://wiki.ros.org/urdf/Tutorials) - Essential for understanding robot description.

## 2. Simulation Environments (Gazebo, Unity, Isaac Sim)

*   **Gazebo:**
    *   Koenig, N., & Howard, A. (2004). *Design and use of Gazebo, an open-source multi-robot simulator*. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS).
    *   **Official Gazebo Documentation:** [https://classic.gazebosim.org/](https://classic.gazebosim.org/)
*   **Unity:**
    *   **Unity Robotics Hub:** [https://github.com/Unity-Technologies/Unity-Robotics-Hub](https://github.com/Unity-Technologies/Unity-Robotics-Hub) - Contains useful packages for ROS-Unity integration.
    *   **Unity Documentation:** [https://docs.unity3d.com/](https://docs.unity3d.com/)
*   **NVIDIA Isaac Sim & Omniverse:**
    *   **Isaac Sim Documentation:** [https://docs.omniverse.nvidia.com/app_isaacsim/app_isaacsim/index.html](https://docs.omniverse.nvidia.com/app_isaacsim/app_isaacsim/index.html) - Official guide for Isaac Sim.
    *   **NVIDIA Omniverse Documentation:** [https://docs.omniverse.nvidia.com/](https://docs.omniverse.nvidia.com/) - Explore the broader Omniverse platform.
    *   **Isaac ROS Documentation:** [https://nvidia-isaac-ros.github.io/](https://nvidia-isaac-ros.github.io/) - For hardware-accelerated ROS 2 packages.

## 3. Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning in Robotics

*   **Deep Learning & Reinforcement Learning:**
    *   Goodfellow, I., Bengio, Y., & Courville, A. (2016). *Deep Learning*. MIT Press. (A foundational text for deep learning).
    *   Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G. (2018). *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction*. MIT Press. (The classic text for RL).
    *   Levine, S. (2020). *Reinforcement Learning and Control*. University of California, Berkeley. (Online course materials and lectures are highly recommended).
*   **Computer Vision & SLAM:**
    *   Hartley, R., & Zisserman, A. (2003). *Multiple View Geometry in Computer Vision*. Cambridge University Press. (A classic for geometric computer vision).
    *   Cadena, C., Neira, J., Campos, C., Tardos, J. D., & Davison, A. J. (2016). *Past, Present, and Future of Simultaneous Localization and Mapping: Toward the Robust-Perception Age*. IEEE Transactions on Robotics, 32(5), 1309-1335. (Comprehensive review on SLAM).

## 4. Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Systems & LLMs

*   **Large Language Models (LLMs):**
    *   Brown, T. B., Mann, B., Ryder, N., Subbiah, M., Kaplan, J., Dhariwal, P., ... & Amodei, D. (2020). *Language Models are Few-Shot Learners*. Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems, 33, 1877-1901. (Introduced GPT-3, foundational for LLMs).
    *   **OpenAI Whisper:** [https://openai.com/research/whisper](https://openai.com/research/whisper) - Official project page and research paper.
*   **VLA & Embodied AI:**
    *   Recent research papers from conferences like NeurIPS, ICLR, ICRA, IROS focusing on embodied AI, multimodal learning, and robot language grounding. Searching for terms like "Vision-Language-Action," "Embodied AI with LLMs," or "Robot Language Grounding" on Google Scholar or arXiv is highly recommended.

## 5. Humanoid Robotics Specifics

*   **Books/Papers:**
    *   Nakamura, Y. (1991). *Advanced Robotics: Redundancy and Optimization*. Addison-Wesley. (Covers kinematics and dynamics of advanced robots, including humanoids).
    *   Many research papers focusing on bipedal locomotion, balance control, and humanoid manipulation from leading robotics labs (e.g., Boston Dynamics, Google DeepMind, University of Tokyo's JSK Lab).

## 6. General Computer Science & Engineering Principles

*   **Software Engineering:**
    *   McConnell, S. (2004). *Code Complete: A Practical Handbook of Software Construction*. Microsoft Press. (For writing high-quality code).
    *   Martin, R. C. (2018). *Clean Architecture: A Craftsman's Guide to Software Structure and Design*. Prentice Hall. (For structuring maintainable systems).

---

Remember to consult these resources to deepen your understanding beyond the scope of this book. The field of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics is vast and continuously evolving, and continuous learning is key to staying at the forefront.
